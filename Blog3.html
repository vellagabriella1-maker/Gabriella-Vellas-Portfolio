Reflecting on Audience Experience and Its Connection to Media Concepts

Image from https://pixabay.com/photos/social-media-facebook-twitter-1795578/ Part of an Audience: One of the times where i have been part of a audience is where Bridgeton was trending through social media. Many accounts displayed the popular scenes, outfits and intimate moments. I notice many people talking about the show and recommending it through the comments. I became curious and decided to watch the series which was worth the hype. After watching the series, I dived into further on social media the edits and discussions of the show. Positive and Negative of Being Part of an Audience: With the show being aired on social media, my experience like other have been shaped positively and negatively. Through positively, the show being aired on social media made the excitement and anticipation greater than discovering it through the streaming service. Also, the hype of Bridgeton felt like a online community discussing the characters, scenes and even edits. Lastly, the hype brought onto social media brought out a show that I personally might have not discovered otherwise. Flipping it to the other side, some scenes where spoiled through the videos without any option of spoiler alert provided before. Also, sometimes shows hyped up through social media have a greater expectation that gets brought down by the actual show. Importance of this Experience: This experience has shaped how social media can significantly influence viewing choices of shows and movies across the web. Also, this experience has demonstrated how audiences are encouraged to watch content mainly through what is trending at the time. This can also shape if the trend is played too much, watching the show or movie in the future can become a type of cringe and hard to watch. My Experience Connecting to the Week 2 Lecture: This experience relates to the week 2 lecture through how audiences are influenced by media promotion and trends on social media. Also highlighting the power of algorithms developed for social media and how the influence is significantly impacted. Lastly, it shows how modern audiences engage with and share content on what they watch, influencing others to join along. Academic Source Connection: This experience relates to an academic source, David Gauntlet "Ten things wrong with the media effects model" (Gauntlett 1998). David states that media effect model wrongly assumes media directly controls peoples behavior. Though shown through my experience, audiences actively choose what to watch rather than simply being controlled by media content. (Gauntlett 1998) David also states that media audiences interpret content in different ways which connects to how other people posted their own edits and discussion about the show. (Gauntlett 1998). References: Gauntlett, D 1998, Ten Things Wrong With The Media Effects Model, CABI Digital Library, Leicester.

Connotation and Sign: The image above represents a well-known Halloween-themed advertisement used by both Pepsi and Coca-Cola. In both versions of the advertisement, a Pepsi can is depicted wearing a Coca-Cola cape, suggesting that it is disguised as Coca-Cola. At first glance, the image may appear to be a playful Halloween joke. However, a closer look to the ad reveals a deeper layer of competitive messaging between the two brands. In the Pepsi version of the advertisement, the text reads, “We wish you a scary Halloween.” This phrase implies that Coca-Cola represents something frightening, portraying the Coca-Cola costume as a villain or monster when compared with Pepsi. In contrast, the Coca-Cola response to this advertisement reframes the same visual with the caption, “Everybody wants to be a hero.” This demonstrates that the Coca-Cola cape is a superhero costume rather than a villain’s disguise. Through these two ads, Coca-Cola transforms the original ad to suggest that Pepsi wants to be a hero like Coca-Cola. What could these Ads mean? Both of these ads demonstrate the competition between the two through humor. As creators, they brought more competition to each side through the slight change of the text. But shown to the audience, it's both the competition and the vibe for the holiday of Halloween too. Is it Possible to see this Image in More than One Way? Both of these ads can be perceived in more than one way. On the surface and at a glance these ads just seem like a funny bit for the holiday. Though someone can perceive this ad as a form of harm to one's belief. This is because some people are more loving towards one brand and hating the other brand. For some people, this ad could seem like an attack on their beliefs especially if this ad strikes a conversation on how that person's favorite drink is bad compared to the competitor. Though many people will just see this as a funny ad and a competition between the two companies and nothing more. These ads are shown through the Aristotle Model of communication as many people have their own views and beliefs which can impact the effect on each individual. https://www.communicationtheory.org/aristotle%E2%80%99s-communication-model/.

Museum Website of Old Social Media Profiles

Visit website: https://xtdfhddju.my.canva.site/arciveofus. My digital artifact is an interactive website in the shape of a museum which displays abandoned social media profiles and platforms. This website highlights the forgotten stories, designs and posts that occur with the fast changing social media. This website also gives a chance to bring back those profiles into the light one last time while also useful for academics to study the pattern and effects of social media. The above images showcase the layout and aesthetics of the website to the most current version. The main page is a museum outside with instructions on movement, then each clickable area is the guide through the museum showcasing artifacts that are clickable, the top bar showcases about tab, home page and finally a page to add your own social media profiles. I began my project by focusing on using Canva to build a basic website structure with working buttons. At this stage, my goal was simply to ensure navigation worked. However, I quickly realised that the visual design was lacking as it appeared flat and unengaging. To solve this, I experimented with invisible navigation buttons to simulate walking through a space. This shift improved interactivity but introduced new challenges, particularly in creating a visually appealing website. I tested multiple textures and references but many appeared pixelated or distorted which required further adjustments. Another key issue arose when creating the artwork for the museum. I initially considered using real social media profiles but this raised concerns around privacy and copyright. As a result, I adapted my approach by creating fake profiles inspired by real patterns seen across platforms like TikTok and discontinued apps such as Musical.ly. These changes significantly shaped the final outcome leading to a more pleasing and interactive design that better reflects the museum website. he main concept this project connects to is Participatory Culture which describes the media we see are not just for viewing but also carries our voices and views. One specific text reflects this view is Rosen, J 'The people formerly known as the audience' in 2006. Rosen states that we shift from media consumption to active participants. In my interactive museum website, my viewers are engaging with the exploration of these profiles. Though, in the future these viewers will transform into creators with the addition of their own profiles. My website along with the text demonstrates that modern media is no longer one- sided but instead users contribute, interact and share this creativity.
